Rhinoplasty

Rhinoplasty, commonly referred to as a nose job, is a surgical procedure designed to improve the appearance and functionality of the nose. This procedure can address aesthetic concerns, breathing difficulties, and reconstructive needs following trauma or injury.

What is Rhinoplasty?

Rhinoplasty involves reshaping the bone and cartilage of the nose to create a balanced facial appearance. It’s a highly customizable procedure tailored to meet each patient’s unique needs, whether for cosmetic reasons or to address medical issues such as a deviated septum.

Why Choose Rhinoplasty?

  • Improve facial harmony and proportions.
  • Correct functional issues like breathing difficulties.
  • Restore the nose’s appearance after injury or trauma.
  • Boost self-confidence through improved aesthetics.

The Procedure

The surgery typically lasts between 1 to 3 hours and is performed under general anesthesia. During the procedure, your surgeon will reshape the bone and cartilage according to the pre-determined plan. Incisions are strategically placed to minimize visible scarring, and the recovery process begins immediately post-surgery.

Recovery Timeline

  • Day 1-2: Initial swelling and bruising; rest is crucial.
  • Week 1: Nasal splints are removed; swelling begins to subside.
  • Week 2: Most external signs of surgery fade, and you can return to light activities.
  • Months 3-6: Swelling reduces significantly; final results become visible.

FAQs

Q: Will I have visible scars?
A: Most incisions are made inside the nostrils or along natural creases, leaving minimal to no visible scarring.

Q: How soon can I return to work?
A: Most patients can return to non-strenuous work within 1-2 weeks, depending on their recovery progress.

Q: How long does swelling last after rhinoplasty?
A: Swelling can last several weeks, but most will subside within 2-3 months. Final results may take up to a year to fully appear.

Q: Can rhinoplasty fix a deviated septum?
A: Yes, rhinoplasty can correct a deviated septum, improving both the appearance and functionality of the nose.

Q: Is rhinoplasty painful?
A: Most patients report minimal pain during recovery. Discomfort is typically managed with prescribed pain medication.

Q: At what age can I get rhinoplasty?
A: Rhinoplasty is generally recommended for patients over 16-18 years old, once nasal growth is complete.

Q: How long do rhinoplasty results last?
A: Rhinoplasty results are permanent, though natural aging and changes in facial structure over time may slightly alter the appearance.

Q: Can I wear glasses after rhinoplasty?
A: You should avoid wearing glasses or sunglasses that rest on the bridge of your nose for at least 4-6 weeks to prevent pressure on the healing area.

Q: How should I sleep after rhinoplasty?
A: Sleep on your back with your head elevated using pillows to reduce swelling and protect the nose during recovery.

Q: Can rhinoplasty change my voice?
A: Voice changes are rare, but minor alterations can occur if nasal resonance is affected during the procedure.

Q: What’s the difference between open and closed rhinoplasty?
A: Open rhinoplasty involves a small external incision at the base of the nose, while closed rhinoplasty uses internal incisions only. Your surgeon will determine the best approach for your needs.

Q: Can I combine rhinoplasty with other procedures?
A: Yes, rhinoplasty is often combined with procedures like chin augmentation or a facelift to achieve overall facial balance.
